Exploration One

Lymphangioleiomyomatosis A Review

Mohammed Amine Bouanzoul, MD; Yale Rosen, MD

Archives of Pathology & Laboratory Medicine, 2024, 149(8): 775-788

Systenatically summarizes the current situation of lymphangioleiomyomatosis study, with its symptoms and therapies. Lymphangioleiomyomatosis is characterised by a proliferation of smooth muscle–like cells. These cells carry mutations in one or both tuberous sclerosis (TSC) genes. Symptoms of LAM range from exertional dyspnea and coughing to chest pain and recurrent pneumothorax.

Details

LAM is a rare multisystem disorder belonging to the family of neoplasms exhibiting perivascular epithelioid differentiation.The disease is progressive and probably evolves to parenchymal destruction and the formation of cysts.
LAM can occur at any age, and it has a common factor that tuberous sclerosis(TSC) genes mutate. TSC is an autosomal dominant inherited disorder.TSC-LAM appears to be more common than sporadic-LAM(S-LAM),in which S-LAM exhibits TSC2 gene mutations and TSC-LAM demonstrates that both TSC1 and TSC2 mutate. TSC1 encodes hamartin and TSC2 encodes tuberin.These two proteins form a hetero-oligomer that works as a tumor suppressor complex to control mTOR. If the tuberin and hamartin complex is stimulated by growth factors, it will undergo phosphorylation, resulting in a reduction of its GTPase activity. This will activate RHEB, ultimately activating mTOR by binding to raptor and other GPCRs.
Several hypotheses indicated that LAM cells originate from AMLs,lymphatic or bone marrow cells,neural crest cells(NCCs), and even uterine or ovarian cells. LAM cells are also capable of spreading to lung allografts in posttransplant patients.
Extrogens can be a risk factor for LAM. It can lead to invasiveness and destruction of lung parenchyma.LAM patients also have increased blood levels of prolactin.
LAM cells can evade the immune system using receptors like programmed death ligand-1(PD-L1).LAM cells also exhibit NKG2DL downregulated expression,leading to weak recognition by NK cells.
Recurrences of pneumothorax are frequent and strongly suggestive of LAM.Chylothorax is also a common clinical manifestation of LAM,in which the lymphatic fluid leaks into the pleural cavity.This may be due to a disruption or blockage of the thoracic duct or one of its branches.
Most radiologic assessments for LAM are performed when patients exhibit progressive dyspnea or recurrent episodes of pneumothorax.HRCT of the chest is the preferred imaging modality for evaluating patients with suspected LAM.
The panel includes VEGF-D testing, testing for a1-antitrypsin deficiency, serum protein electrophoresis and free light-chain quantification for light chain deposition disease,and autoantibody testing for autoimmune diseases including rheumatoid factor, antinuclear antibodies, and Sj ̈ogren-related antibodies. A VEGF-D level lower than 800 pg/mL cannot be used to exclude the diagnosis of LAM.Only serum VEGF-D testing has clinical utility in LAM.
The usefulness of bronchoscopy for diagnosis in patients with LAM remains a topic of debate. Bronchoscopy is generally not needed in cases where clinical suspicion of LAM is high.
LAM is characterized by the presence of cysts of variable sizes and a proliferation of immature smooth muscle–like LAM cells that line the cysts, often in a micronodular pattern.Type II pneumocyte hyperplasia and destruction of elastic fibers and collagen are frequent findings.HMB45 is the current gold standard immunostain used to confirm the diagnosis of LAM.Cathepsin k can also be a marker for LAM cells according to recent studies.The lungs of male and female patients with TSC may exhibit a distinctive lesion called multifocal multinodular pneumocyte hyperplasia(MMNPH).
Sirolimus has demonstrated efficacy in reducing lung function decline, improving quality of life, enhancing functional performance, and reducing VEGF-D levels. Everolimus has also demonstrated efficacy in treating AMLs, central nervous system tumors, and epilepsy in patients with TSC.These drugs have some side effects such as mouth ulcers, diarrhea, nausea, increased blood cholesterol, skin rashes, swelling of extremities, pneumonitis, ovarian cyst formation, and Pneumocystis jirovecii infection. Lung transplantation has emerged as the optimal approach in patients with severely altered functional status.

Exploration Two

Nintedanib for patients with lymphangioleiomyomatosis: a phase 2, open-label, single-arm study

Sergio Harari, Davide Elia, Antonella Caminati, Jens Geginat, Francesca Luisi, Giuseppe Pelosi, Claudia Specchia, Olga Torre, Roberta Trevisan, Chiara Vasco, Maurizio Zompatori, Roberto Cassandro

The Lancet Respiratory Medicine, 2024, 12(12): 967-974

Experiments suggest that nintedanib can be a second-line therapy in patients not controlled by standard treatment with mTOR inhibitors. Forced Expiratory Volume in the 1st second(FEV1) remains stable after 12 months of treatment with nintedanib, while it declines slightly during 12 months off the treatment.The most frequent adverse events include nausea, diarrhoea, and abdominal pain.

Concepts

MILES trials: Multicenter International LAM Efficacy of Sirolimus, reveal that sirolimus can control the LAM but not cure.
A phase 2, open-label, single-arm study: focus on drug efficacy and dose,without control groups, contrary to double-blind.
RESULT trials: Use sirolimus and resveratrol("combined therapy"), reveal that VEGFD-levels are downregulated, life quality improves and strangely FEV1 declines slightly.

Limitations

Single-arm study cannot confirm the efficacy of nintedanib(whether is is the fluctuationc of the disease itself);
Open-label design probably impact the judgement when it comes to questionnaires and even measurements of lung functions;
Sample size is small, resulting a broad confidence interval;
Moreover, experimental subjects include newly treated patients and Patients who have failed sirolimus treatment. Their mTOR sensitivity differ.

Exploration Three

Novel treatment strategies for lymphangioleiomyomatosis: a narrative review

Davide Elia, Sergio Harari, Lu Fan, Rémi Diesler and Elizabeth P. Henske

European Respiratory Review, 2025, 34(177):

This review demonstrates the pathological mechanisms and relevant therapies of LAM.

Concepts

4E-BP1: eukaryotic translation initiation factor 4E-binding protein 1;
Akt: protein kinase B;
B7-H3: B7 homologue 3;
CQ: chloroquine;
CTLA-4: cytotoxic T-lymphocyte-associated protein 4;
E2: oestrogen;
HCQ: hydroxychloroquine;
MDK: midkine;
mTOR: mechanistic target of rapamycin complex;
PD-1: programmed cell death protein 1;
PD-L1: programmed death-ligand 1;
PI3K: phosphoinositide 3-kinases;
Rheb: Ras homologue enriched in brain;
Rho-A: Ras homologue family member A;
RTK: receptors of tyrosine kinases;
S6K1: ribosomal protein S6 kinase beta-1;
Src: proto-oncogene tyrosine-protein kinase Src;
TSC: tuberous sclerosis complex;
Ulk1: Unc-51 like kinase 1

Pathology

LAM is a multisystmeic disease characterized by the presense of perivascular epithelioid cells, also called LAM cells. The TSC2 protein acts as a GTPase, maintaining Rheb in an inactive, guanosine diphosphate-bound form. Phosphorylation of TSC2 by Akt inactivates the TSC complex, promoting the association of mTOR with Raptor to form the mTORC1 complex. This process will upregulate the downstream proteins p70S6K and 4E-BP1, resulting in increased expression of proteases and vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF). Moreover, mTORC1 phosphorylates and inhibits the transcription factor E (TFE) B and TFE3, further inhibiting autophagy.
mTORC binds to Rictor, leading to the mTORC2 complex, which will activate the Akt and promote LAM cell survival.mTORC2 can also activate RhoA, influencing cytoskeletal rearrangement and cell motility.
In short, while mTORC1 plays as the primary driver of LAM, mTORC2 promotes disease progression through cytoskeletal regulation, migration and survival pathways.
Hyperactive mTORC1 signaling results in enhancement of metabolism and increased expression of angiogenic factors (VEGF-A) [16] and lymphangiogenic growth factors (VEGF-C and VEGF-D). Importantly, in the context of TSC2 deficiency, the transcription factors TFE3 and TFEB move to the nucleus, where they drive the expression of proteases including cathepsin K.
However, according to recent studies, S6K and 4E-BP1 are regarded as canoncial targets of mTORC1, while TFEB and TFE3 are regarded as noncanonical targets.
In general, rapamycin inhibits the phosphorylation of S6K and 4E-BP1, but increases the phosphorylation of TFEB and TFE3. This results in translocation of TFEB and TFE3 from the nucleus to the cytoplasm, where they are sequestered by 14-3-3 proteins.mTORC1 is considered a master inhibitory regulator of autophagy via several mechanisms, which include direct phosphorylation and inhibition of the Ulk1/2 kinases and direct phosphorylation of the TFEB and TFE3 transcription factors, leading to their exclusion from the nucleus via binding to cytoplasmic 14-3-3 proteins.

Therapies

The use of sirolimus for LAM is approved by US Food and Drug Administration (FDA), European Medicines Agency (EMA) and drug regulatory agencies in many other regions
Everolimus is approved by the EMA and FDA for the treatment of TSC-associated sub-ependymal giant cell astrocytoma and AMLs;
Chloroquine (CQ): an inhibitor of autophagy, blocks lysosome-autophagosome fusion and lysosomal protein degradation;
Resveratrol seems to counterbalance the increased autophagy induced by rapamycin and, thus, promote apoptosis in TSC-deficient cells;
RTKs are surface receptors that receive and transfer growth factors. Treatment with anti-epidermal growth factor receptor antibodies can reduce the number and size of pulmonary alternations, lymphangiogenesis and angiogenesis.
Binding of VEGF-D to VEGR3 promotes lymphangioogenesis and tumour angiogenesis, improving tumour growth or metastatic spread. Axatinib can block the VEGF-D signaling and inhibit the growth of TSC2-null tumours;
Nintedanib is a dose-dependent inhibitor of the fibroblast growth factor (FGF), VEGF and PDGF signalling pathways;
To downregulate oestrogen, triptorelin acts as a synthetic analogue of the gonadotrophin-releasing hormone;

Challenges and Future

Even under the best of circumstances, it will be difficult to prove that a therapy is better than sirolimus;
Immune checkpoint blockade therapy: treatment with anti-PD1 and anti-cytotoxic T-lymphocyte-associated protein 4(CTLA-4);
In mouse models of TSC, inhibition of MDK has synergistic benefit when combined with rapamycin;
Loratadine; histamine H1 receptor inhibitor: reduce the growth of TSC2-null subcutaneous tumours;
Saracatinib, selective Src inhibitor: decrease epithelial-mesenchymal transition, migration, and invasiveness in TSC2-null cells;

Exploration Four

Modulation of infiltrating CD206-positive macrophages restricts progression of pulmonary lymphangioleiomyomatosis (LAM)

Heng-Jia Liu, Remi Diesler, Joelle Chami, Boxiang Wu, Heng Du, Yue Jin, Melissa Daou, Nicola Alesi, Damir Khabibullin, Caroline Leroux, Vincent Cottin, Lloyd G. Cantley, Udo Rudloff, Elizabeth P. Henske

European Respiratory Journal, 2025,

Markers

LAM cells: PMEL, VEGFD, ESR1,
Macrophages: CD68, MARCO, F4/80(mouse),
M2 Macrophages: CD206/MRC1, ARG1, CD163, TREM2, CTSC, IL10, TGFB1,
M1 Macrophages: TNF-α, NLRP3,
Alveolar Macrophages: MARCO, MCEMAP1, TREM2, ABHD5, PPARG, RETN, MRC1,
Interstitial Macrophages: MARCKS, CXCL10,
Immunosuppressive Genes: CD163, MRC1, CD200R1, TGM2, IL1R2, IL-10, TGFβ1, IL1RN, CCL17, CCL22, CCL24,
Macrophage-homing Genes: CXCL12,
RP-182: A 10-mer therapeutic peptide that selectively induces a conformational switch of the mannose receptor CD206 and transforms immunosuppressive into proinflammatory macrophages;

Findings

LAM-associated macrophages display immunosuppressive features;
CD206+ macrophages are near LAM nodules, and LAM cells express macrophage-homing factors. Signaling from MIF to its receptor complex CD74-CD44 is activated in the signal flow from LAM cells to LAM-associated macrophages.LAM-associated alveolar macrophages near the cysts showed increased expression levels of cysteine proteases, including CTSB, CTSL, CTSS, and CTSD;
TSC2-deficient cells induce the expression of immunosuppressive M2-markers in macrophages.TSC2-deficient cells drive M2 macrophage polarization that is mTORC1 dependent;
Treatment with RP-182 increased the fraction of phagocytosed 105K tumor cells in M2-polarized macrophages.

Exploration Five

Small extracellular vesicles and induction of epithelial-tomesenchymal transition in lymphangioleiomyomatosis

Amina Jouida, Lucas Souza Dantas, Donal O’Malley, Evelyn Lynn, Marissa O’Callaghan, Minzhe Guo, Patrick McFadden, Grace Buckley, Michael P. Keane and Cormac McCarthy

European Respiratory Journal, 2025, 66(3)

Background

Matrix metalloproteinases(MMPs), particularly MMP-2 and MMP-9, degrade the extracellular matrix (ECM), facilitating tissue destruction in LAM;
Vimentin: key structural protein that belongs to intermediate filaments, maintaining cell shape and integrity, and also helping cell migration and motility;
Small extracellular vesicles (sEVs) have recently been implicated in promoting epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) in cancer;

Markers

Extracellular Vesicle-specific Markers: CD63, CD81,

Findings

In LAMCORE cells, TWIST1, a transcription factor which induce EMT, is uniquely expressed, as well as another upregulated one, SNAIL2;
MMP2, MMP11 and MMP14 are also upregulated in LAMCORE cells;
Small extracellular vesicles (sEVs) significantly increase in serum of LAM patients, which is also negatively correlated with forced expiratory volume. Furthermore, activity of MMPs (particularly pro-MMP-9) and vimentin expression are significantly upregulated in A549 cells treated with sEV from LAM;
In protein cargos derived from LAM patients, upregulated proteins are linked to the complement cascade, including C3 and C5. Moreover, compstatin, a C3 inhibitor, could significantly reduce the vimentin expression, MMP-9 activity and the invasive capacity of A549 cells induced by sEVs from TSC-deficient cells. It may also suggest that compstatin can be a potential therapeutic strategy for LAM.

Exploration Six

Complement system activation: bridging physiology, pathophysiology, and therapy

Elie Azoulay, Julien Zuber, Ahmed Aziz Bousfiha, Yun Long, Ying Tan, Sushan Luo, Meriem Essafti and Djillali Annane

Intensive Care Medicine, 2024, 50(11):1791-1803

Background

Acitivated complement system involves over 50 proteins in the cascade, via three main pathways (classical, mannan-binding lectin, and alternative). Overactivity of the complement system potentially induces runaway inflammation, damaging to host tissues. Complement system dysregulation is associated with atypical hemolytic and uremic syndrome (aHUS), myasthenia gravis (MG), C3 glomerulopathy, and paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria;

Mechanism of Complement System

Main Function: lysis (of cells, bacteria, or viruses), opsonization (phagocytosis), activation of the inflammatory response, and clearance of immune complexes;
Classical Pathway: triggered by antibody–antigen complexes
Mannan-binding Lectin Pathway: triggered by carbohydrates in bacterial walls
Complement Alternative Pathway: activated non-proteolytically when plasma water hydrolyzes the thioester bond within C3
Three pathways converge to generate C3 convertase and C5 convertase, which induce the release of anaphylatoxins (C3a and C5a) and opsonin (C3b/iC3b). Binding to specific receptors with C3a and C5a will induce the production of inflammatory mediators, stimulating endothelial cells and promoting cell migration and activation. Furthermore, C5b binds with C6,7,8,9 and forms C5b-9, also known as the membrane attack complex (MAC), lysing cells and pathogens;
Complement alternative pathway is always active at a low level and involves spontaneous hydrolysis of C3. This can release small amounts of C3b and help produce the C3 convertase C3bBb;

Regulators for Complement System

Plasma C1 Esterase Inhibitor (C1-INH): inhibits activated-C4 cleavage in the classical and lectin pathways;
Factor I, Factor H, C4BP, and Complement Receptor 1: prevents unwanted C3b/C4b deposition on host cells;
Factor H, C4BP, CD46, Complement Receptor 1, and CD55: increases the dissociation of C3 and C5 convertases on host cells;
CD59, Clusterin, and Vitronectin: prevent MAC insertion on cell surfaces;

Impacts in Critical Illnesses

The complement system is strongly activated in sepsis. The large amounts of the proinflammatory protein C5a released by sepsis induce tissue damage, increase the risk of infection, and enhance blood thrombogenicity;
C3a and C5a increase cytokine levels, leukocyte chemotaxis, and vascular endothelial permeability, thereby contributing to capillary leakage syndrome;
Excessive complement activation may occur in acute respiratory distress syndrome. However, C3-blocker compstatin AMY-101 and complment inhibitor ravulizumab do not siginficantly improve the outcomes of COVID-19;
Bradykinin-related angioedema is associated with complement system;

Potential Drugs

Eculizumab: inhibit C5;(C5 inhibition preserves opsonization by C3b and C4b, as well as immune signaling mediated by C3a, but prevents generation of the highly proinflammatory C5a and MAC)
Ravulizumab: engineered from eculizumab, with longer half-life;
Compstatin AMY-101: inhibit C3;
Pegcetacoplan: inhibit C3
Zilucoplan: inhibit C5;
